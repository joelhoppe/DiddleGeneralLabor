FAQ

Straight answers

The questions that come up most. Don't see yours? Call or text — it's faster than email anyway.

How much does junk removal cost?

Every job's different, so every job gets a free estimate. Price depends mostly on how much there is and how hard it is to get to. Text a photo of the pile to (870) 595-4718 and you'll usually have a number back within the hour.

Do I need to be home when you come?

Nope. As long as I can get to the stuff — garage open, gate unlocked, or pile at the curb — the job gets done and you get a photo when it's finished.

How fast can you get here?

Often same-day or next-day, depending on the schedule. Storm cleanup and rental turnovers on a deadline get priority — say so when you call.

Where does the junk go?

Whatever can be donated or scrapped gets donated or scrapped; the rest goes to proper disposal. Nothing gets dumped where it shouldn't be — that's your name and mine on the line.

Is there anything you won't take?

Hazardous materials — chemicals, paint, asbestos, and the like — have to go through proper channels, so I can't load those. Call anyway and I'll point you to who handles it.

How do I pay?

Cash or check works, and most payment apps do too. Payment's due when the job's done and you're happy with it — not before.

Do you do jobs outside Rector?

All the time. Piggott, Marmaduke, Paragould, Kennett — anywhere in Clay or Greene county is regular territory, and for a good-sized job I'll drive farther. Just ask.

Can you help with just the loading?

Sure can. Plenty of folks rent their own dumpster or truck and just need a strong back for an afternoon. Labor-only jobs are welcome.
